Artist
Statement

My work confronts the failure of society to protect its most vulnerable: children. In a world increasingly shaped by individualism, consumerism, and instant gratification, the needs and voices of children are too often ignored or silenced.

I use painting and printmaking not only as creative expression but as a form of resistance — against apathy, injustice, and the systems that prioritize profit and ego over empathy and care.

I am deeply influenced by the stories I hear in my community — stories that reveal how children are affected by violence, instability, and the absence of protective structures.

I believe art has the power to challenge cultural values and provoke necessary reflection. Each piece asks: Are we truly supporting the next generation, or are we complicit in the erosion of their potential?

"When I create I feel very rational in a way that I escaped without babysitting chaos or from actual absurdity going on around me."

The Artist

Estifanos Solomon

A visual artist from Ethiopia, based in Addis Ababa, exploring themes of passion, conflict, and personal narrative. My artistic journey began at the Russian Centre for Science and Culture (2014), developing foundational skills in art.

I earned a BFA in Printmaking from Addis Ababa University's Alle School of Fine Arts and Design (2022). Through painting and printmaking, I weave storytelling and lived experience into works that invite viewers into deeper emotional and societal reflection.

My practice extends beyond the studio — teaching at art academies and leading workshops at Tinishu Art Studio.
